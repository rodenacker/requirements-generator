Untraceable requirements the main result

Every requirement or citation that traces to nothing — broken citations (a source that cannot be quoted), dropped-but-present content (the consultant rejected it, yet it survived the merge), orphans (no antecedent in the draft or any ledger), not-alignable units (an antecedent may exist but was reworded past confident matching), and — at TIER-1 — unattributed facts. Drift & dead-provenance

Lower-severity warnings, not part of the untraceable headline. Drift — a retained value changed post-merge in a way not explained by a correction (a possible hand-edit off its source). Dead-provenance — a ledger entry whose content is no longer in the final doc (often a legitimate merge drop). The consultant adjudicates.
