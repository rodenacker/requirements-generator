# map-jtbd-from-inputs-to-ui.md

**Purpose:** Translate a JTBD job map produced from raw consultant inputs (`analyse-inputs/JTBD/jtbd-job-map.html`) into signal-based inputs for the design-spec and requirements-drafting consumers. Unlike `map-task-flows-to-ui.md` (decomposition → affordances) or `map-ooux-to-ui.md` (objects → screens), JTBD-from-inputs produces **signals about user motivation surfaced from primary source material** — actors, situations, motivations, outcomes, and the four forces of progress (push / pull / anxiety / habit) — not direct UI affordances. The mapping is therefore: **job catalogue** (every entry in `final_jobs`, with its actor, canonical statement, type, opportunity score and band, and `[SRC: <filename>]` source set) → routed to primary-task-weighting hints for the design-spec consumer (jobs with `band-high` opportunity become candidate primary tasks for their actor; the actor seeds the persona-to-screen routing; the situation seeds the trigger-condition naming for the corresponding screen state); **statement stacks** (the *"When `<situation>`, I want to `<motivation>`, so I can `<outcome>`."* triples) → routed to user-story seeds for `§4 User goals & stories` in the `/requirements` drafter (the drafter normalises the *"As a `<actor>`, I want to `<motivation>` so that `<outcome>`"* form and assigns `R-` IDs); **outcomes carrying `(no-metric-in-inputs)`** → routed to consultant-interview prompts in the requirements-quality consumer (each marker is a question: *"the inputs name this outcome but no measure was anchored; what's the success criterion?"*); **measurable outcomes** → routed to acceptance-criteria seeds for `§6 Functional requirements` (each measure becomes a candidate pass/fail condition; the drafter assigns IDs and merges); **scoring markers `consultant-assigned-no-signal`** → routed to consultant-interview prompts (each marker is a question about Importance or Satisfaction the inputs did not anchor; closing the gap improves the opportunity matrix accuracy); **opportunity matrix** (5×5 grid plotting jobs by `(importance, satisfaction)`) → routed to drafter-priority signals for `§9 Priorities` (jobs in the top-right innovation-opportunity quadrant `imp ≥ 4 ∧ sat ≤ 2` get higher prioritisation; the drafter weights them above jobs in lower-opportunity bands); **forces of progress (Push / Pull / Anxiety / Habit per cluster)** → routed to NFR seeds (`§6.1`, `§6.3`), change-management requirement seeds (Anxiety → onboarding / training requirement), and migration-strategy seeds (Habit → coexistence with current tool); **forces marked `not-named-in-inputs`** → routed to consultant-interview prompts (each absent force is a question the consultant should answer before requirements sign-off — most commonly *"what anxieties do users have about switching?"* and *"what habits will they have to break?"*); **cluster headings (`(actor, main-goal)` pairs)** → routed to feature-cluster entries in the design-spec inventory (each cluster becomes a candidate capability cluster; the main-goal seeds the cluster description; the actor seeds the persona-to-cluster routing); **emotional / social jobs** → routed to UX / microcopy requirement seeds (`§6.4`) for the moments when the actor wants to feel a certain way or be seen a certain way; **the embedded `<!-- jtbd-meta: manifest_fingerprint=… -->` cursor** → used by re-runs to detect drift between the job map and the current manifest; not routed to design consumers.

**Inputs:** `analyse-inputs/JTBD/jtbd-job-map.html`, `framework/assets/pattern-catalogue/` (for primary-task / user-story / acceptance-criteria / NFR / consultant-question / change-management pattern entries when authored).

**Outputs:** Design-spec inventory entries (feature-cluster candidates from cluster headings, primary-task hints from high-opportunity jobs, persona-to-cluster routing from the actor field), requirements-drafter signal entries (user-story seeds for `§4`, acceptance-criteria seeds for `§6` from measurable outcomes, NFR seeds for `§6.1`/`§6.3` from forces, UX / microcopy seeds for `§6.4` from emotional / social jobs, integration / migration seeds from Habit force, prioritisation signals for `§9` from the opportunity matrix), informational entries (`(no-metric-in-inputs)` / `consultant-assigned-no-signal` / `not-named-in-inputs` consultant-interview prompts, manifest fingerprint for drift detection).

**Used by:** `framework/agents/design-spec-drafter/agent.md` (when authored) and any future requirements-quality reviewer that consumes input-analyser output. Also conceptually consumed by the `/requirements` drafter when this artefact is re-ingested via `input/`: HTML classifies as `Native-text` per `framework/skills/classify-input-tier.md` (UTF-8 passes the sniff); the input-handler adds it to the manifest as a new row; the drafter reads its job-card content directly (no markitdown conversion needed). The drafter's `[SRC: C-NNN]` claim IDs cite the JTBD artefact; the JTBD artefact's own `[SRC: <original-filename>]` markers within the cited content trace the audit trail end-to-end through to the original briefs / interview notes / decks that justified the job. The merger retains both citation layers in the final `requirements.md`.

**Used how:** Called when the JTBD-inputs job map is present. Combines with other `map-*-to-ui` skills via the accrue-all pattern — each skill contributes complementary entries to the same downstream inventory. The JTBD-inputs skill focuses on **user-motivation signals, opportunity-derived prioritisation, force-derived NFRs and change-management requirements, and primary-task weighting**; the thematic-analysis skill focuses on **cross-cutting-pattern signals and feature-cluster routing**; the OST skill focuses on **outcome-tree signals and bridge-to-requirement routing**; the journey-mapping skill focuses on **temporal-workflow signals and sentiment-derived prioritisation**; the task-analysis skill focuses on **decomposition-derived information-requirement signals and Plan-derived acceptance-criteria branches**. JTBD on raw inputs never authors UI; it only surfaces signals other consumers anchor to. The analyser is **re-ingestible by `/requirements`** as a fresh source: the drafter reads its statement stacks as user-story seeds (`§4`), its measurable outcomes as acceptance-criteria seeds (`§6`), its emotional / social jobs as UX / microcopy seeds (`§6.4`), its Push / Pull as NFR / value-proposition signals, its Anxiety as change-management / onboarding seeds, its Habit as migration-strategy seeds, and its opportunity matrix as prioritisation signals (`§9`). The audit trail from the requirements draft back to the original brief is preserved through the dual-citation chain (drafter's `[SRC: C-NNN]` → JTBD map's `[SRC: <filename>]`).

**Note on the requirements-doc-lensing sibling skill (`framework/skills/map-jtbd-to-ui.md`):** that skill is a stub for the `/analyse-requirement` JTBD analyser, whose input is the synthesised `requirements/requirements.md` and whose provenance markers are section-anchored (`provenance-from-personas`, `src-from-task-flows`, etc.). This skill cannot be reused as-is because its input contract is filename-anchored and its `[SRC: <filename>]` markers preserve a different audit-trail shape (raw-input filenames vs synthesised-doc section anchors). The two stubs can be merged later if/when both reach beyond-stub status and prove source-agnostic; until then, keep them parallel.
