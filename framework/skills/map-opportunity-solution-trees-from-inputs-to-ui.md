# map-opportunity-solution-trees-from-inputs-to-ui.md

**Purpose:** Translate an inputs-side Opportunity Solution Tree (`analyses/inputs/OPPORTUNITY-SOLUTION-TREES/opportunity-solution-tree.md`) into signal-based inputs for the design and requirements-quality pipelines. Unlike `map-task-flows-to-ui.md` (which maps decomposition → affordances) or `map-ooux-to-ui.md` (which maps objects → screens), the inputs-side OST produces **signals about the strategic discovery ladder the raw inputs already imply**, not direct UI affordances — analyses are extraction; design and consolidation are downstream concerns. The mapping is therefore: **primary Outcome** → routed to product-strategy alignment signals for design-spec consumers (the Outcome anchors the design's value proposition); **candidate Outcomes** (`[CANDIDATE-OUTCOME]` markers) → routed to stakeholder-alignment conversations (each candidate is a strategic option the consultant deferred at first run; the design-spec consumer surfaces them so the consultant can confirm the primary choice still holds at design time); **Opportunity catalogue** (every entry in `final_tree.opportunities`, with its canonical `<actor> — <need clause>`, its supporting `[SRC: <filename>]` set, and its laddering edge to the primary Outcome) → routed to feature-cluster seeds in the design-spec inventory (each Opportunity is a potential cluster of related capabilities; the Opportunity label seeds the cluster name; the `need_clause` seeds the cluster description; the `[SRC: <filename>]` set anchors the cluster to its evidence trail); **Candidate-requirement lines** (every bullet under `## Candidate requirements`, shaped *"The system should `<verb> <object>` so that `<outcome>`"*) → routed to acceptance-criteria seeds for the `/requirements` drafter (same channel as `thematic-analysis`'s `Theme-to-requirement-candidates` bridge; the drafter normalises voice, assigns `R-NN` IDs, and merges into `§6` of `requirements/requirements-draft.md`; `[SRC: <filename>]` markers from the analysis coexist with the drafter's `[SRC: C-NNN]` claim IDs in the draft and are stripped by the merger at requirements-finalisation time); **Orphan solutions** (every entry in `## Coverage diagnostics > Orphan solutions`) → routed to consultant-interview prompts in the requirements-quality consumer (each orphan is a question the consultant should answer before `/requirements` runs — *"the inputs name solution `S-N` but no source-grounded opportunity it addresses; what need does it serve, or should it be de-scoped?"*); **Unaddressed opportunities** (every entry in `## Coverage diagnostics > Unaddressed opportunities`, each carrying the `[UNADDRESSED]` flag) → routed to consultant-interview prompts (*"the inputs name opportunity `Op-N` but commit no solution to it; should it be deferred, out-of-scope, or does it imply a missing feature?"*); **Weakly-anchored opportunities** (every entry in `## Coverage diagnostics > Weakly-anchored opportunities`, each carrying the `[WEAKLY-ANCHORED]` flag) → routed to consultant-interview prompts (*"opportunity `Op-N` doesn't keyword-match the primary Outcome; is it relevant, or does it indicate the primary Outcome should be re-picked?"*); **Contradictions** (every entry in `## Coverage diagnostics > Contradictions`) → routed to consultant-interview prompts (*"opportunities `Op-A` and `Op-B` carry opposing need clauses for the same actor; which represents the authoritative requirement, or do both need accommodating?"*); **Assumption Tests** (every entry in `final_tree.assumption_tests`, classified by Torres's five categories `desirability` / `viability` / `feasibility` / `usability` / `ethical`) → routed to validation-plan signals for the design-spec consumer (each test is a hypothesis the team should validate before committing the design; absent Layer 4 — the expected state for raw inputs — emits a single advisory entry suggesting the consultant brief the team on what to validate when assumption tests are absent from the source material); **Multi-parent Solutions** (Solutions that semantically address ≥ 2 Opportunities) → flagged for design-spec consumers as candidates for over-loaded-feature review (one feature serving two distinct customer needs may signal scope conflation); **Manifest fingerprint** (the `<!-- ost-meta: manifest_fingerprint=… -->` cursor) → used by re-runs to detect drift between the analysis and the current manifest; not routed to design consumers.

**Inputs:** `analyses/inputs/OPPORTUNITY-SOLUTION-TREES/opportunity-solution-tree.md`, `framework/assets/pattern-catalogue/` (for feature-cluster / acceptance-criteria / consultant-question pattern entries when authored).

**Outputs:** Design-spec inventory entries (feature-cluster candidates, validation-plan signals, over-loaded-feature flags, stakeholder-alignment prompts), requirements-quality signal entries (acceptance-criteria seeds, consultant-interview prompts for orphans / unaddressed / weakly-anchored / contradictions), informational entries (manifest fingerprint, absent Layer 4 advisory).

**Used by:** `framework/agents/design-spec-drafter/agent.md` (when authored) and any future requirements-quality reviewer that consumes input-analyser output. Also conceptually consumed by the `/requirements` drafter when this artefact is re-ingested via `input/` (drafter reads the bridge section as candidate-requirement seeds, with the analysis's `[SRC: <filename>]` markers superseded by the drafter's `[SRC: C-NNN]` claim IDs in the draft).

**Used how:** Called when the inputs-side OST artefact is present. Combines with other `map-*-to-ui` skills via the accrue-all pattern — each skill contributes complementary entries to the same downstream inventory. The OST inputs-side skill focuses on **strategic-ladder signals and feature-cluster routing**; the `thematic-analysis` skill focuses on **cross-cutting-pattern signals and theme-based cluster routing**; the `glossary` (future) skill focuses on **vocabulary-health signals**; the `jtbd` (future) skill focuses on **outcome-statement and job-to-screen signals**; the `five-whys` (future) skill focuses on **justification-health signals**. OST never authors UI; it only surfaces signals other consumers anchor to. The analyser is also one of two input-analysers (the other being `thematic-analysis`) that can be **re-ingested by `/requirements`** as a fresh source: dropping `analyses/inputs/OPPORTUNITY-SOLUTION-TREES/opportunity-solution-tree.md` into `input/` classifies it as `Native-text`; the drafter reads its `## Candidate requirements` section as candidate-requirement seeds and its `[SRC: <filename>]` markers as in-document citations (which the merger strips alongside its own `[SRC: C-NNN]` IDs).

**Distinct from the requirements-side OST map skill.** `framework/skills/map-opportunity-solution-trees-to-ui.md` exists for the reverse-discovery sibling under `/analyse-requirement` and maps audit-style findings (orphan Solutions, unaddressed Opportunities, vertical-only branches, weakly-anchored Opportunities) into design-quality / requirements-quality signals. The inputs-side variant is structurally similar but operates on **forward-discovery** signals (the load-bearing addition is the `## Candidate requirements` bridge to the `/requirements` drafter) and uses **file-based** rather than **requirement-id-based** source anchoring. Per the registry doc's clause on map-skill naming, the inputs-side variant uses the `-from-inputs-to-ui` suffix to distinguish it; both files coexist on disk.
