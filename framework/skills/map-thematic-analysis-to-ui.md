# map-thematic-analysis-to-ui.md

**Purpose:** Translate a thematic analysis (`analyse-inputs/THEMATIC-ANALYSIS/thematic-analysis.md`) into signal-based inputs for the design and requirements-quality pipelines. Unlike `map-task-flows-to-ui.md` (which maps decomposition → affordances) or `map-ooux-to-ui.md` (which maps objects → screens), Thematic Analysis produces **signals about cross-cutting patterns in the raw inputs**, not direct UI affordances — analyses are extraction; design and consolidation are downstream concerns. The mapping is therefore: **theme catalogue** (every entry in `final_themes`, with its 3–6-word label, 1–2-sentence definition, and supporting code list) → routed to feature-cluster entries in the design-spec inventory (each theme becomes a candidate cluster of related capabilities; the theme label seeds the cluster name; the definition seeds the cluster description; the supporting `[SRC: <filename>]` set anchors the cluster to its evidence trail); **theme-to-requirement-candidates** (every line in the bridge section, shaped *"The system should `<verb> <object>` so that `<outcome>`"*) → routed to acceptance-criteria seeds for the `/requirements` drafter (the drafter normalises voice, assigns `R-` IDs, and merges into `§6`; `[SRC: <filename>]` markers from the analysis coexist with the drafter's `[SRC: C-NNN]` claim IDs in the draft and are stripped by the merger); **coverage gaps** (every `[GAP-DEDUCTIVE: <concern>]` marker in the Diagnostics section) → routed to consultant-interview prompts in the requirements-quality consumer (each gap is a question the consultant should answer before `/requirements` runs or before sign-off — *"the inputs hint at <concern> but no theme is supported; do you want to add more material covering it, or accept it as out of scope?"*); **silent concerns** (concerns in the 10-area frame with no source mentions) → recorded as informational entries only, not routed (a silent concern means the consultant did not surface the topic; the right action is consultant judgement, not a routed flag); **cross-source themes** (`cross_source: true` in the theme record) → flagged for design-spec consumers as higher-confidence clusters (a theme corroborated across two or more sources is more reliable than a single-source theme); **theme-map proximity edges** (the dashed `T1 -.-> T2` edges in the Mermaid diagram, indicating themes that share ≥ 30% of codes but were kept distinct) → flagged for design-spec consumers as candidates for cluster consolidation review (the consultant decides whether the proximity warrants a single combined cluster or stays as two distinct clusters with cross-links); **manifest fingerprint** (the `<!-- thematic-meta: manifest_fingerprint=… -->` cursor) → used by re-runs to detect drift between the analysis and the current manifest; not routed to design consumers.

**Inputs:** `analyse-inputs/THEMATIC-ANALYSIS/thematic-analysis.md`, `framework/assets/pattern-catalogue/` (for feature-cluster / acceptance-criteria / consultant-question pattern entries when authored).

**Outputs:** Design-spec inventory entries (feature-cluster candidates, cluster-consolidation flags), requirements-quality signal entries (acceptance-criteria seeds, consultant-interview prompts for coverage gaps), informational entries (silent concerns, manifest fingerprint).

**Used by:** `framework/agents/design-spec-drafter/agent.md` (when authored) and any future requirements-quality reviewer that consumes input-analyser output. Also conceptually consumed by the `/requirements` drafter when this artefact is re-ingested via `input/` (drafter reads the bridge section as candidate-requirement seeds, with the analysis's `[SRC: <filename>]` markers superseded by the drafter's `[SRC: C-NNN]` claim IDs in the draft).

**Used how:** Called when the thematic analysis is present. Combines with other `map-*-to-ui` skills via the accrue-all pattern — each skill contributes complementary entries to the same downstream inventory. The thematic-analysis skill focuses on **cross-cutting-pattern signals and feature-cluster routing**; the glossary skill focuses on **vocabulary-health signals and label-source routing**; the jtbd skill focuses on **outcome-statement signals and job-to-screen routing**; the five-whys skill focuses on **justification-health signals and policy/KPI pattern routing**. Thematic analysis never authors UI; it only surfaces signals other consumers anchor to. The analyser is also the only input-analyser that can be **re-ingested by `/requirements`** as a fresh source: dropping `analyse-inputs/THEMATIC-ANALYSIS/thematic-analysis.md` into `input/` classifies it as `Native-text`; the drafter reads its bridge-table candidate-requirement lines as seeds and its `[SRC: <filename>]` markers as in-document citations (which the merger strips alongside its own `[SRC: C-NNN]` IDs).
